💡 Key Insights
1. Mentoring is the Standout Service
Mentoring emerged as the most sought-after service across the dataset, reflecting a strong and consistent customer preference for personalised, one-on-one career guidance over self-directed resources.
2. Steady Engagement Across Supporting Services
Interview Preparation, LinkedIn Profile Optimisation, and Networking services all showed steady and reliable engagement levels, indicating a healthy multi-service demand profile rather than over-reliance on a single offering.
3. Broad National Market with Key State Leaders
Customer participation is distributed relatively evenly across Australian states, with NSW, VIC, and NT showing slightly higher volumes. This signals a scalable national market with no single geographic dependency — a positive indicator for sustainable growth.

✅ Business Implications

Demand Forecasting — The model can reliably predict future service uptake, allowing the business to anticipate demand spikes and avoid under or over-resourcing
Staffing & Operations — Predictive outputs can directly inform mentor availability, scheduling, and hiring decisions ahead of peak periods
Marketing Prioritisation — High-demand services like Mentoring should anchor campaign messaging, with Interview Prep and LinkedIn services positioned as complementary upsells
Geographic Expansion — Even participation across states suggests the business model is scalable nationally without needing region-specific product adjustments
